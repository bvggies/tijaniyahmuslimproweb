Frequently Asked Questions

Aqiqah is a Sunnah practice in Islam involving the sacrifice of an animal (typically one or two goats/sheep) as an expression of gratitude to Allah for the birth of a child. It is usually performed on the 7th, 14th, or 21st day after birth, but can also be done later if needed

Aqiqah is not obligatory but is a highly recommended Sunnah. It was practiced by the Prophet Muhammad peace be upon him, who performed Aqiqah for his grandsons, Al-Hasan and Al-Husain. Many scholars regard it as a strongly encouraged act that carries a significant spiritual reward.

Aqiqah serves multiple spiritual and social purposes:

  • It expresses gratitude to Allah for the blessing of a child.
  • It acts as a form of protection for the child from harm.
  • It symbolizes a child's closeness to Allah from an early age.

Some scholars also view it as a form of charity, which can bring blessings to the family and community.

In Islam, Aqiqah is done on the 7th day after a child's birth. However, if that is not possible, it may be performed later, such as on the 14th or 21st day, or any time thereafter. The intention and sincerity matter more than the timing.
