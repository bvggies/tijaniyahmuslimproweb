Palestine’s Reminder: 6 Prophetic Lessons on Protecting Children

  • Siti Zahidah
  • 30 October 2025
  • Community
بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمٰنِ الرَّحِيْمِ
In the name of God, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

The Heart of Mercy

Mercy is at the center of faith. Allah reminds us, “My mercy encompasses all things” (Surah Al-A’raf, 7:156). Among those who deserve that mercy most are children. Their innocence, laughter, and hope are blessings, and their protection is a divine responsibility.

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ showed mercy in every word and action. Protecting children is not only an act of kindness but a command from faith itself. Today, as the world watches the suffering of Palestinian children in the Gaza Strip, these teachings remind us of our duty to care and to act.

1. Mercy Is the Heart of Faith

The Prophet ﷺ said, “He is not of us who does not show mercy to our young and respect to our elders” (Sunan al-Tirmidhi).

Mercy is what completes a believer’s character. The Prophet ﷺ did not limit his compassion to his own family or community. He smiled at children, prayed for them, and showed that every child deserves dignity.

When Muslims extend this mercy to all children, including those living under the Palestine flag, we live by the values he taught.

2. The Prophet’s Actions Spoke of Love

The Prophet’s tenderness was seen in the smallest moments. He kissed his grandsons Hasan and Husayn, carried his granddaughter Umamah while leading prayer, and shortened his prayer when he heard a baby cry.

At a time when such affection was uncommon, he made love and gentleness the foundation of leadership.

Parents and teachers today can follow his example by showing patience, listening carefully, and treating every child with compassion. These small acts build hearts that know mercy.

3. Children Are an Amanah from Allah

In Islam, children are a trust from Allah. The Qur’an says, “Let those fear [for their own children] who, if they left behind weak offspring, would be afraid for them” (Surah An-Nisa, 4:9).

Caring for children is part of our worship. Their wellbeing, safety, and education are responsibilities we cannot ignore. When a child is hurt or neglected, it weakens the entire community.

Protecting children in every land, from Jakarta to Jerusalem, is a reflection of our faith and a sign of our gratitude to the One who entrusted them to us.

4. Mercy Begins at Home

The home is where mercy takes root. When parents speak kindly, share stories of the Prophet’s compassion, and encourage gentle behavior, they raise children who carry mercy into the world.

Simple moments — helping a neighbor, comforting a friend, sharing food — teach children to value kindness. These habits create Muslims who care about others, including children living in hardship in Palestine and Gaza.

You can also use the Muslim Pro app to set reminders for daily Qur’an reflection or read prophetic stories together as a family.

5. The Test of Our Time: Mercy in Palestine

The suffering of Palestinian children is a test of our conscience. In the Gaza Strip, many face fear, loss, and displacement, yet their courage and hope continue to inspire the world.

The Palestine flag stands for endurance, while the ancient Palestine olive tree reminds us of faith that remains firm despite hardship. Sharing Palestine food at community gatherings or fundraisers helps us remember that their lives are more than conflict — they are lives of culture, family, and faith.

True mercy requires action. We can make sincere dua, speak out for justice, and give through verified Palestine donation campaigns focused on children’s wellbeing.

6. Following the Prophet’s Path Means Protecting the Young

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ led with mercy because he knew that a nation without compassion for its children will lose its soul. Protecting the young is a reflection of our love for Allah and His Messenger.

Every believer who feeds a hungry child, comforts the scared, or teaches the unprotected continues the Prophet’s mission. Mercy is not only a feeling; it is an action that shapes the world.

Faith in Action: What We Can Do

  • Pray with Intention. Dedicate time daily to make dua for children living in danger everywhere, especially in Palestine and Gaza. Ask Allah to grant them safety, healing, and peace.
  • Educate Your Family. Teach your children about the Prophet’s kindness and the importance of standing for justice. Use resources to explain global issues in an age-appropriate way.
  • Support Trustworthy Charities. Donate to well-established, transparent charities that provide direct aid to children in conflict zones. Look for organizations that deliver food, healthcare, clean water, and educational resources.
  • Speak Up Peacefully. Use your voice to advocate for the protection of all children. Share verified information, write to elected officials, and support initiatives that call for peace and justice.
  • Build Habits of Giving. Use the Muslim Pro app to plan your daily prayers or set a recurring reminder to give sadaqah (charity) regularly, creating a sustainable habit of generosity.

Even small acts of mercy can make a difference when they come from a sincere heart.

A Prophetic Legacy of Protection

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was sent as a mercy to all creation. His example teaches that true faith shines through kindness, especially toward the young.

By protecting children in our homes and standing for those in Palestine, we keep his message alive.

May Allah make us people of mercy, protect every child, and fill the hearts of believers with compassion and strength. Ameen.
